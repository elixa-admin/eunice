# South African School Intake Comparison

Date: 2026-05-22

This note compares Eunice’s intake process against public admissions flows from SPARK Schools, Reddam House Constantia, Michaelhouse, and St Stithians College. The goal is not to copy their process, but to extract patterns worth adopting, avoid weak patterns, and keep Eunice’s unique requirements intact.

## Schools Reviewed

- SPARK Schools primary application flow and admissions guidance
- Reddam House Constantia admissions process and enquiry/tour flow
- Michaelhouse admissions process and registration form
- St Stithians College admissions policy and process guidance

## What They Do Well

### 1. They separate the journey into clear stages

- Reddam and Michaelhouse both make the process feel like a sequence, not a single long form.
- SPARK is especially explicit about the path: application, payment, document submission, contract, confirmation.
- St Stithians frames admissions as a managed process with timelines, assessments, and acceptance windows.

### 2. They set expectations early

- SPARK states what happens next, including payer details, documents, and contract signing.
- Michaelhouse publishes timelines, events, and decision windows.
- Reddam tells families about enquiry, tour, document upload, interview, acceptance, and induction.

### 3. They use human support as part of the product

- Reddam leans into personal tours and admissions team help.
- Michaelhouse highlights named admissions staff and information events.
- St Stithians positions a central admissions office as the point of contact.

### 4. They collect the right family and fee context

- Michaelhouse asks for detailed parent/guardian information, including identity, marital status, addresses, and occupation.
- St Stithians requires both parents’ details and consent, or proof of guardian authority.
- SPARK distinguishes primary caregiver, account payer, and scholar.

### 5. They treat documents as conditional, not all the same

- SPARK clearly separates core docs from non-citizen requirements and contract docs.
- Reddam lists core uploaded files before review and interview.
- Michaelhouse and St Stithians rely on academic reports, interviews, and family context to shape later steps.

## Patterns Worth Adopting for Eunice

- A staged admissions journey with visible progress.
- Clear “what happens next” messaging after each step.
- A named admissions support path, not just a generic contact form.
- Distinct family roles:
  - submitting parent
  - legal guardian
  - fee payer
  - secondary parent or co-parent
- Conditional document rules based on applicant context.
- A short decision timeline summary after submission.
- A tour / meeting / interview step where appropriate, rather than forcing all families into one rigid flow.

## Patterns We Should Avoid Copying Directly

- Overloading the first form with too much administrative weight.
- Forcing every family into the same document checklist, even when circumstances differ.
- Making the process look like a payment ledger before the parent understands the school value proposition.
- Hiding the human side of admissions behind only forms and uploads.

## How This Changes Eunice

Eunice should keep its unique requirements, but the application experience should now feel more like a guided admissions journey:

- A strong landing page that explains the path before data entry starts.
- A stepwise parent flow with milestones and reassurance.
- Conditional uploads and role-aware prompts.
- A clean handoff from application to review, interview, and decision.
- Better support for family arrangements and fee responsibility.

## Product Ideas to Carry Forward

- A progress/timeline component that shows the full admissions journey.
- A “what happens next” card after each major action.
- An admissions support card with contact details and response expectations.
- A conditional document checklist that updates live as the parent answers questions.
- A school-specific configuration layer so the same product can serve other schools later.
